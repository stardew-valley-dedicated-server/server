# TestOverlay geometry is a pixel-coordinate contract with RenderingTests (and the ffmpeg burn-in)

`TestOverlay.cs` (mod, net6.0) draws a white panel at known coordinates. `RenderingTests.cs` (tests, net10.0) samples pixels at those coordinates to detect whether rendering is active. The two files target different .NET versions with different references and cannot share constants via project ref or `<Compile Link>`. Before editing either side — geometry, font, padding, color, or sample location — read the other file and confirm the contract still holds.

**The recorder's ffmpeg burn-in is a third party to this contract.** `ContainerRecorder.BurnInFilter` draws `TIME {epoch}` (`fontsize=24`, black, `box=0`, at `x=8/y=5`) into the white top row `TestOverlay` reserves (`ReservedPtsWidth` wide, `RowHeight`=28 tall) — flush-left on the same `TextInsetX` and on the same baseline band as the in-game `TICK`/`NAME` rows (`RowHeight` = `Game1.smallFont.LineSpacing`), so the burn-in, tick counter, and farmer name read as one panel with even row spacing. This couples three files across the net6.0 / net10.0 / ffmpeg-string boundary with no shared symbol. The in-game side paints the top row white *first* and ffmpeg adds only black glyphs (no box), so pixel `(0,0)` stays white. Two ways to break it: shrinking `RowHeight`/`ReservedPtsWidth` so the row no longer backs the burn-in, or moving the ffmpeg `x`/`y` toward the corner (or re-adding `box=1`) so black reaches `(0,0)`. Changing `fontsize=24` on the recorder side without resizing the row breaks it too. (Burn-in labels are space-separated, not `TIME:` — a colon in drawtext `text=` breaks ffmpeg's avfilter parser; see `BurnInFilter`'s doc-comment.)

**Why:** A prior session adjusted the overlay (smaller font, shrunk padding 6→4, bold black text replacing red, but kept a 2px outer margin) without checking what RenderingTests sampled. The test's 3×3 sample grid at (8..14, 8..14) had previously sat in the white background; after the adjustment the grid landed directly on the bold-text strokes of "T:", brightness fell below the 200 threshold, and `ServerApi_SetRendering_Enable_ScreenShouldHaveContent` failed even though rendering was working correctly. The contract is invisible to the compiler — there is no shared symbol to break.

**How to apply:** When editing `TestOverlay.cs`, open `RenderingTests.cs` and verify the panel still produces a white pixel at `PanelOrigin` (currently `(0, 0)`). When editing `RenderingTests.cs`'s sample logic, mirror any change in `PanelOrigin` / sampled-pixel choice with a matching comment update on the overlay side. When editing `ContainerRecorder.BurnInFilter`'s `x`/`y`/`fontsize`/`box`, confirm the burn-in still lands inside `TestOverlay`'s reserved top row and that pixel `(0,0)` stays white. The cross-reference comments on each `PanelOrigin` constant and on `TextInsetX`/`RowHeight`/`ReservedPtsWidth` ↔ `BurnInFilter` are the only drift detector — preserve them. Adding a new row, changing the font, reintroducing a margin, or moving the burn-in all break the contract; treat any geometry change as cross-file by default.
